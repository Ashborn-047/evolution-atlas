The Evolution of Chroma

Before we manipulate the spectrum, we must understand how color shaped the digital frontier.

1995: The Safe Colors

In the era of 8-bit monitors and CRT screens, designers were limited to 216 "Web Safe" colors. Design was dictated by hardware limitations. High contrast neon text on black backgrounds wasn't just a style; it was a necessity for legibility on flickering screens.

<body bgcolor="#000000">

2005: Skeuomorphism

With the rise of Web 2.0 and the iPhone, color was used to mimic reality. Gradients, drop shadows, and glossy reflections taught users how to interact with digital interfaces by making them look like physical buttons. Color served the purpose of affordance.

2013: The Flat Revolution

Apple's iOS 7 and Google's Material Design stripped away the textures. We entered an era of bold, solid colors and minimalism. Color became the primary tool for hierarchy, removing the noise to focus purely on content and typography.

2025+: Adaptive Systems

Today, color is dynamic. Semantic tokens, dark mode adaptation, and generative palettes (like Material You) mean color is no longer static; it reacts to the user's environment and preferences. We design systems, not just pages.
